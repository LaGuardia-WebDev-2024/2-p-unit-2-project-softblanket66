Shanghai

Must eat dishes!

Xiao Long Bao (Soup Dumplings)
ground pork seasoned with a little ginger and Shaoxing wine mixed with a gelatinized
pork stock that melts on cooking, transforming into a rich, sticky soup.

Sheng Jian Bao
Fried dumpling, the bottoms are crisped and browned. The whole panful is doused with a bowl of water
and fitted with a heavy wooden lid until the tops of the dumplings are steam-cooked. As the
water evaporates, the bottom gets an extra dose of heat to seal in the crisp crust just before serving.

Shansi Leng Mian (Eel Noodles)
First, fine wheat noodles, a little flat rather than round, served cold so they have a firmness
to the bite, with a splash of light brown vinegar on the bottom and a slick of sesame sauce on
the top. Second is the eels, by way of contrast served hot, swimming in the most marvelous sweet,
oily, gingery, soy braising liquid.

Scallion oil noodles (cong you ban mian)
Fine hand-pulled noodles dressed with scallion oil (made by frying julienned scallions
until they become dark, crisp shreds), mixed with soy sauce, and topped with the fried scallions, along with
some fried and dried shrimp.
